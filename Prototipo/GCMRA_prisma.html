Attitude Control of a Satellite using a CMG as Only Actuator

A 5 degree of freedom mechanical system is shown. Two degrees of freedom corresponding to the satellite (\(x\) and \(y\) coordinates) are controlled by two degrees of freedom CMG.

Geometric algebra and Euler-Lagrange approaches have been used to model the system. Within the framework of robot equations

\(M \ddot{q} + C\dot{q} = \tau \),

control techniques can be applied to the system, without relying on exogenous tools such as quaternions

In this animation we see how the satellite orient to the reference position \(q^{ref}_x = 89^\circ \), \(q^{ref}_y = 89^\circ\) while the remaining coordinate \(q_z\) tend to a constant.
